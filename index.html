I'm Milou, an Artistic Design Researcher.

My interests center around the philosophy of technology and design, prompting fundamental questions about the role of technology in our lives, the ethics of progress, and the impact of design on human experiences.

I'm driven by curiousity to understand what well-being means. Through artistic explorations I hope to contribute positively to the world we share.

Vision on Design

When I observe the society I am an inseparable part of, I find it impossible to paint an objective picture. My vision for design is rooted in the belief that it covers every facet of our existence. And that design serve as embodied expressions of our collective Soul that reveal the universal laws of life

I see design as a powerful tool to foster deeper understanding and connection—both within ourselves and with the world around us. By focusing on the nuances and subjectivities that define our human experience, design can initiate real transformation. For me, design holds the potential to bridge gaps, dissolve polarizations, and cultivate a more harmonious and compassionate society. I advocate for a balanced worldview that recognizes both the challenges and the possibilities of our future, advocating for holistic approaches, global collaboration, and ethical technological advancements

I believe that design should inspire us to harmonize body, mind, and spirit, encouraging a reconciliation with our humanity. Are we calm, courageous and wise enough to surrender to our to the unknown? Technology, when used thoughtfully, can be a means to this end, helping us to reconcile with our human nature rather than alienate us from it. Inspired by intercultural philosophies, I envision a world where design prompts us to pause, reflect, and engage with profound questions about our existence and our future.

Ultimately, my goal is to design joyfull experiences that serve as invitations for self-discovery and collective growth, fostering a sense of fulfillment and well-being in an ever-evolving world.

Professional Identity

I think of myself as an Artistic Design Researcher with a solid foundation in design theory and practice.

I am pursuing a master degree in Industrial Design at the Eindhoven University of Technology, and I hold a bachelor degree in Industrial Design Engineering from the Rotterdam University of Applied Sciences.

My professional and educational background allowed me to develop a diverse skill set and a broad perspective on design, demonstrating my ability to work within different contexts and with various people. It also shows how my practice has evolved from stakeholder-driven and product-driven projects to more conceptual and speculative designs, reflecting a deep interest in well-being and the ethical implications of technology.

My Research through Design approach, often described as 'poetic, is characterized by an explorative attitude where I get inspired by diverse philosophies and materials. I use my sensitivity, focus, child-like openness, technical insight, and practical skills to bring my ideas to life. This method allows me to reflect on emerging technologies and create physical, interactive and intelligent research probes that are both aesthetically and conceptually engaging.

    General Reflection on Experiences and Education

    My experiences at Vetus, where I developed various boating parts, required collaboration across various disciplines and cultures, focusing strongly on technology and realization. My time at Philips Research, where I designed a medical device for spinal fusion surgery. involved working on cutting-edge technologies in real-life contexts, providing insights into user interactions and aesthetic dimensions. Both experiences equipped me with practical product design skills and experience of working in the field

    After some time of working I was eager to learn more, gain international experience, work on societal projects, and explore the "why" rather than the "how" or "what

    I didn't fully know what I signed up for when I ambitiously applied for the Industrial Design pre-master program. Luckily, I immediately felt I had made the right decision. The first course I followed was "Design for Debate", which set the tone for an incredible intellectual and personal journey in domain of speculative design

    My recent internship in Stockholm, at RISE Research Institutes of Sweden allowed me to live abroad, learn about new cultures, work in a consortium of individuals with different expertise, and taught me to put design research methodologies into practice. During my internship I delved deeper into the concepts of digital ethics through the lens of slowness, intimacy, and compassion, areas of research that are often overlooked within international organizations I’ve been involved with, but have been recognized as significant elements for the workforce. As such, this experience contributed to acknowledging the relevance of my interest in the actuality of a business organization

    Additionally, during my master’s, I started working as a tree worker, caring whole bodily for the green environment around us. This experience profoundly transformed my relationship with materials and my perspective on innovation. It invited me to contemplate the value of maintenance and care

    Reflecting on my Master’s program at TU/e, I noticed how I gradually adapted to the demands of academia, which were initially quite new to me. Unexpectedly, it became a period to explore my fascination with moral emotions and the richness of (human-computer) interaction

    I got particularly enthusiastic about examining how artificial agents influence moral emotions such as compassion, gratitude, and awe, aligning with my passion for exploring how technology can enhance well-being and cultivate positive emotional development. This interest is exemplified in my Final Master Project, "Adore: An Opening to Contemplation," which builds on your theory of Interactional Morality and aims to learn more about the influence of embodiment.

    The common thread I noticed, is that through the lens of well-being I have gradually narrowed my focus on meaningmaking, morality, ethics, spatial design and embodiment
